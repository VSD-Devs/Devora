## Search intent

This search is usually made by a business comparing local suppliers. The buyer wants confidence, not a list of buzzwords.

## Start with commercial fit

Ask whether the agency understands how your website should produce value. A good Sheffield web design partner should ask about enquiries, customers, services, locations, proof and what is not working on the current site.

## Questions to ask

- Will the site have dedicated service pages?
- How will local SEO be handled?
- Who writes or structures the copy?
- What happens to existing URLs during a redesign?
- Will metadata and schema be server-rendered?
- Can the site be expanded with case studies and articles later?
- How is mobile usability tested?

## Warning signs

Be cautious if the conversation jumps straight to colours, templates or page counts. Those things matter, but they should follow the strategy.

Also be cautious if every page sounds the same. Local SEO pages need useful context, internal links and visible content that helps buyers.

## What good looks like

A strong website partner will connect design, development, SEO and conversion. They will talk about site structure, content hierarchy, performance and how visitors move from search result to enquiry.

For Sheffield businesses, that usually means a strong homepage, clear [web design Sheffield](/services/web-design) and [local SEO Sheffield](/services/local-seo) pages, a useful [Sheffield area page](/areas-we-cover/sheffield), relevant case studies and a simple contact route.

## FAQs

### Should I choose a local agency?

Local knowledge helps, especially for Sheffield and South Yorkshire search intent. The more important factor is whether the team can build a site that is useful, fast and commercially clear.

### How many agencies should I speak to?

Two or three is usually enough. More than that can make comparison harder unless you have a very clear brief.
