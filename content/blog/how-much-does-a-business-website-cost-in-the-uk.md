## Search intent

People searching this topic usually want a realistic budget before speaking to a web design business. The useful answer is not a single number. It is an explanation of what changes the cost and what a business should expect at each level.

## The short answer

A serious UK business website can range from a few thousand pounds for a focused small business site to significantly more for a custom build with deeper content, integrations, ecommerce, area pages, technical SEO and ongoing iteration.

The cheapest quote is rarely the cheapest outcome if the site is slow, thin, hard to update or unable to generate enquiries.

## What affects the cost?

- Number of page types
- Depth of discovery, positioning and copy planning
- Bespoke design versus template adaptation
- Custom development and integrations
- CMS or content editing requirements
- SEO architecture, metadata, schema and redirects
- Ecommerce, booking, job board or portal functionality
- Testing, launch planning and support

## What should a proper quote include?

A useful quote should explain the sitemap, deliverables, assumptions, content responsibilities, revision process, technical stack, launch support and any ongoing costs.

If a quote only says “five-page website”, ask what those pages are expected to achieve.

## FAQs

### Is a cheap website ever enough?

Sometimes, for a very early proof of concept. For a serious service business, the site still needs clear positioning, fast loading, credible design and a simple enquiry journey.

### Should I pay monthly or upfront?

Both can work. The important question is what you own, what support is included and whether the site can be moved or improved later.

### Does SEO cost extra?

Technical SEO foundations should be part of a professional build. Ongoing SEO, content and authority building are usually separate.
